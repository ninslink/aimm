Abstract

Timely assessments of child acute malnutrition are essential for effective treatment and prevention of undernutrition. We developed a simple smartphone app, D2A, to enable regular self-collection of Family MUAC and key household drivers of wasting by mothers and caregivers. Based on a seven-month pilot study with 180 households, we explore the acceptance, accuracy, and cost of app-based self-collection of Family MUAC with and without the assistance of Community Health Volunteers relative to paper-based nutrition screenings.

Abstract

Insight into the resilience of local food systems is critical for the treatment and prevention of child acute malnutrition. We use latent class mixed modeling and time-to-event analysis to develop and test a measure of resilience that is outcome-based, sensitive to shocks and stressors, and captures the enduring effects of how frequently and severely children face the risk of acute malnutrition.

Trajectories of resilience to acute malnutrition in the Kenyan drylands

DOIPublisher
Abstract

Anticipating those most at-risk of being acutely malnourished shapes decisions about resource allocation and intervention in times of food crises. Using a household dataset from 23 Kenyan counties from 2016 to 2020, we seed, calibrate, and validate an evidence-driven computational model to explore how variation in household behavior influences vulnerability to malnutrition.
