D2A: a community-led smartphone tool for malnutrition screening in Kenya
Bhavnani, R., & Link, N. S. (2026). Frontiers in Public Health.
DOI Publisher page Views: —
Abstract
Timely assessments of child acute malnutrition are essential for effective treatment and prevention. The paper introduces D2A, a smartphone workflow to enable regular self-collection of Family MUAC and key correlates of wasting.
Trajectories of resilience to acute malnutrition in the Kenyan drylands
Bhavnani, R., Schlager, N., Reul, M., & Donnay, K. (2023). Frontiers in Sustainable Food Systems.
DOI Publisher page Views: —
Abstract
The study develops an outcome-based measure of resilience to acute malnutrition using longitudinal data from Kenya and links distinct trajectories to differential household risk.
Household behavior and vulnerability to acute malnutrition in Kenya
Bhavnani, R., Schlager, N., Donnay, K., Reul, M., Schenker, L., Stauffer, M., & Patel, T. (2023). Humanities & Social Sciences Communications.
DOI Publisher page Views: —
Abstract
Using a large household dataset from Kenya, the paper studies how adaptive capacity affects vulnerability to acute malnutrition and highlights heterogeneity in crisis responses.
