D2A: a community-led smartphone tool for malnutrition screening in Kenya

Bhavnani, R., & Link, N. S. Frontiers in Public Health.

Timely assessments of child acute malnutrition are essential for effective treatment and prevention. The paper introduces D2A, a smartphone workflow to enable regular self-collection of Family MUAC and key correlates of wasting. A seven-month pilot with 180 households is used to evaluate acceptance, accuracy, and costs relative to paper-based screenings.

Trajectories of resilience to acute malnutrition in the Kenyan drylands

Bhavnani, R., Schlager, N., Reul, M., & Donnay, K. Frontiers in Sustainable Food Systems.

The study develops an outcome-based measure of resilience to acute malnutrition using longitudinal data from Kenya between 2016 and 2020. It identifies distinct trajectories across wards and links them to differential household risk, underscoring the importance of measuring resilience at fine geographic scales.

Household behavior and vulnerability to acute malnutrition in Kenya

Bhavnani, R., Schlager, N., Donnay, K., Reul, M., Schenker, L., Stauffer, M., & Patel, T. Humanities & Social Sciences Communications.

Using a large household dataset from Kenya, the paper calibrates a computational model and runs counterfactual experiments to study how adaptive capacity affects vulnerability to acute malnutrition. The analysis highlights heterogeneity in crisis responses and shows how household behavior can shape nutritional risk under stress.
