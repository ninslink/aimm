Project

Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition

A smartphone-based approach for detecting and monitoring child acute malnutrition in low-resource settings.

Accelerating progress toward Sustainable Development Goal 2 requires access to granular, low-cost, and near-real-time data on children’s nutritional status. Current data collection efforts are often fragmented, infrequent, costly, and inaccessible, particularly during crises or in remote areas, limiting their effectiveness in driving timely intervention and targeted support.

The Artificial Intelligence for Monitoring Malnutrition project introduces a low-cost, household-operated tool for monitoring undernutrition using image-based AI for real-time nutrition classification without the need for physical scales or measuring tapes.

Implemented in the Indian state of Maharashtra in collaboration with the Center for Artificial Intelligence at FLAME University in Pune, AIMM focuses on the development, validation, and deployment of an accessible nutrition monitoring workflow.

Objectives

  1. Refine deep learning models that estimate key anthropometric indicators such as weight, height, MUAC, and weight-for-height z-scores from 2D and infrared images.
  2. Provide a lower-cost, real-time alternative to conventional survey-based nutrition monitoring.
  3. Enable caregivers and communities to collect nutrition data using a non-invasive, user-friendly mobile application.
  4. Support adaptation across contexts through an open-source design that can integrate into broader health systems.
